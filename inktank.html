The Problem

The problem that this app, INK TANK, is solving for is to minimize the time wasted while searing for the exact tattoo, tattoo artist, or tattoo studio. Once the search is optimized, the user will be happier with their results, and thus diminish the feeling of regret once the user has gotten their tattoo.

Problem Statement

Tattoo seekers need a way to research tattoo styles, artists, and studios within one app because the current resources are scattered and are not cohesive in their information which creates a randomized, time-consuming experience. We will know this to be true when we see tattoo enthusiasts using this app, INK TANK, as their sole information source from the beginning to the end of their tattoo journey.

UX Competitive Analysis

In order to understand the scope of INK TANK, a competitive analysis was conducted on two similiar apps, Inkstinct, Inc. and TATTOODO. A SWOT Profile was then created for each app. This analysis found the ways in which the competitor apps were winning, where they needed improvements, and where INK TANK would be able to excel in its design. 

Insights

-Inkstinct has the basic foundation of what a tattoo seeker may want, but the app needs better organization and refinement. 
-Tattoodo has a great gallery of images but the inability to combine a tattoo style and a keyword in a search negatively impacts the ability to have precise results.

User Interviews

With my problem statement in mind, I conducted three separate interviews with my target audience - those who have gotten tattoos before and who also research tattoos online. These interviews set out to accomplish two goals:
1) Understand how tattoo seekers explore inspiration for tattoos. 
2) Determine what’s missing from their current tattoo search methods and tattoo community.   

Affinity Mapping

The participants' answers were gathered from these user interviews and were organized by topic in the affinity map below. Their insights helped to shape the app by getting to know who would benefit from INK TANK and what needs would be met with the design. 

Insights

-Security and trust are very important when booking an appointment. The ability to communicate prior to an appointment will help to ease any worries. 
-Referrals, filters, and sharing images within the app are helpful to the user in finding the right Artist for their tattoo idea. 

User Personas

The user insights then led to focusing on user personas which helped to create empathy in the design. The personas' goals were kept at front of mind and assisted in prioritizing their most important needs first. One of those needs, finding and saving an image for later, is documented in Nicholas's mental model below. 

Insights

-Nicholas represents the main persona of INK TANK. He doesn't have much downtime and needs a quick and easy way to see the top rated tattoo Artists in his area.
-Nicholas needs to use a distance feature, read reviews, and see the Artists' relevant work.
-The second persona, Alicia, can be found here. She enjoys reading about the tattoo industry and would enjoy articles of the day. Alicia needs a way to communicate with her Artists of interest before she visits their shop in order to feel safe.

Card Sorting and Sitemap

Once the main objectives of the personas were identified, the first sitemap was drafted. The sitemap was then tested with a card sorting activity with five volunteers. The card sort led to restructuring the initial layout, and the second iteration of the sitemap was then determined as a success.  

Wireframes & Iterative Design

Once the sitemap was in action, the fundamental designing began. INK TANK's design has been through several development stages including pen and paper, low, mid, and high-fidelity wireframes. Each iteration saw improvements due to gaining more familiarity with resources like Material Design, iOS Human Interface Guidelines, and Responsive Grids.

Insights

Two key functions of the app that proved to have the biggest gains were the Search tool and the Appointments page. Now they are simplified at first sight, with the ability to accomplish more in less time due to faster understanding on behalf of the user. 

Solutions

Through the iterations, solutions were created for the personas and users alike. A detailed search feature is a key element of INK TANK and what sets it apart from the competition. A clean layout paired with sorting and filtering options are displayed below. 

The next flow was created from the problem of security with the persona, Alicia, in mind. Users needed a solution for the feeling of safety when making an appointment through INK TANK. Therefore, the ability to message with an Artist became an important feature and one that also assists in nailing down a tattoo design idea with image sharing, font selection, and voice assistance. 

Usability Test

Wireframing brought INK TANK to life and it was ready for user feedback with a clickable prototype. I conducted usability tests with six participants. The goal of the usability tests was to observe the overall level of satisfaction the user has with the app, as well as their ability to complete three specific tasks. Usability testing was what shaped the user flow and design the most as the questions and comments flowed through Moderated Remote tests using Zoom to record the conversations. 

Insights

-The biggest finding from the first prototype was that the cognitive load was too extensive because of the amount of options displayed on each page. After considering what the user would ultimately desire, the design became more concise and thus more functional in the current prototype.
-The Affinity Maps below were created following the testing to see quotes and commonalities.

Next Steps

The goal of INK TANK is to be a worldwide tattoo destination on a daily basis for tattoo enthusiasts as well as tattoo Artists and Studios. Without the Artists and Studios using the app, it will not reach its maximum ability to be mutually beneficial to all users. Furthermore, this problem would cause a sharp decrease in the usage. I would like to test my hypotheses by first finishing out the enthusiasts’ portion of the app, and then beginning to design the Artist and Studio side. I predict that once the app is able to accommodate the needs of all three parties, INK TANK will be the go-to destination for all tattoo interests. The previous steps taken such as user flows, card sorting, and usability tests will all resurface throughout this process.
