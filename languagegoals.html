LANGUAGE GOALS

The Problem

The problem that this app, Language Goals, is solving for is to compete with a lack of motivation to form reachable goals when learning a new language. This objective can be overwhelming and language goals provides a space to create quantifiable goals with a nudge from notifications and alarms to keep users on track.

Problem Statement

The Proto Persona, Abbey, needs a way to be held accountable to her Spanish language studies because she relies on social media to occupy her downtime rather than striving towards her goals. We will know this to be true when we see Abbey studying in Language Goals for 30 minutes a day.

UX Competitive Analysis

I conducted an analysis on three competitor apps that have the purpose of language learning. These apps were Chegg Prep, Quizlet, and Flash Cards. I generated feedback for each after using the apps and used their advantages and disadvantages to inspire my own creation. Straight-forward design with pre-generated tools were the basis for Language Goals. I also incorporated ideas that would benefit the persona such as a timer to focus on the daily goal of studying for “x” minutes a day.

User Interviews

Interviews with existing app users for language learning were completed over the phone. The major takeaways were their feelings when using these apps such as: 
1) I feel proud when I see how much time I've spent using a self-improvement app.
2) I like encouragement, accountability, and goal plans and I want to see more of that in an app.
3) I feel bored learning from a textbook so an app is a better way for me to stay engaged.

User Persona

From the User Interviews, I was able to accumulate their similarities and formed one porto-persona named Abbey.

User Flows

Two main tasks for the app were determined and user flows were then formed. These two tasks were to a customize a game to play, and to create a goal.

Wireframing & Prototyping

Low and Mid fidelity wireframes resulted from the user flows and began with pen and paper, to then be transferred into Marvel.

Usability Testing

Three participants were invited to complete four tasks within the Language Goals clickable prototype. Their results varied in the Jakob Nielsen's Error Severity Rating scale from 0 to 4. Improvements were then made to adjust the most severe errors.

Next Steps

If I were to continue finalizing Language Goals, my next steps would be:
1) Build out “Current Goals” - create a checklist of to-do’s for the user to check off as the goal is getting accomplished
2) Incorporate more color - more difference between black and white was requested
3) Add labels to navigation bar - this will aid users in switching screens faster
4) Test again - test users again to see the new outcome
